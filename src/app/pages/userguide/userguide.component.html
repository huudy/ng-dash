PURPOSE OF THE TOOL

SONAR (Sales Opportunities and Transactions for Markets) is a cross asset tool, covering FX and FX derivatives, Interest Rate derivatives, Commodity derivatives, Equity derivatives and Investment products (all asset classes; including deposits) that provides sales people in CTS a personalized insight to and smart use of Markets' data. The purpose is to have a data-driven detection and visualization of sales opportunities, along with customization and optimization of risk, efficiency and product offering.

GENERAL FRONTEND

The general frontend of SONAR starts with an overview of the Dashboard. It is possible to get a holistic view of the whole Sales team portfolio of clients by clicking on the "Team View" button from the drop-down menu on the top, as well as other available geographical subdivisions.

To dive deep into a specific client it is enough to start typing the name, the NDG or the matchcode of the client in the search bar on the top-right corner and a drop-down menu will appear with the first matchings. Click on the client name or on the search lens to be redirected to the Client View section where you will find more details.

  • In the search bar not only is possible to search a client from the drop-down menu but also to look-up and to go to some specific subpages in SONAR (e.g. Market Data). The search also contains links to specific sections in this user guide.

By clicking on the question mark button available throughout SONAR, the SONAR User Guide will open up and provide further guidance.

The User Guide opens directly into the area related to the section where the "?" button was clicked. For further support please drop an email to or call the number of one of the contacts in the Contacts section .

In general, some common features are available for any table in SONAR. See the example below:

  • On the top left, it is possible to choose the number of entries to be shown (e.g. 10-100) from a drop-down menu. This item is hidden in tables that have less than 10 entries.
  • By hovering over a table, the option of exporting the table to Excel or or generating a print friendly version.
  • Some tables also feature a switch that can toggle between data from different time frames to be displayed in the table.
  • The search bar can be used to filter the table. Multiple search terms can be added to the filter by separating them with a space, e.g. "Options USD". The terms may occur in any of the tables columns. If you want to filter for keywords that contain a space use quotation marks ("") around your search term.
  • To sort a table click on the respective column's header.
  • Below the table you can find the pagination controls that display how many items are currently visible and allow to toggle between the different pages of the table. The toggle is hidden in tables that only have a single page.

DASHBOARDS AND STATISTICS

Dashboard

The dashboard can be accessed by clicking the UniCredit / SONAR icon in the top navigation bar.

The dashboard summarizes the most important information on your portfolio of clients. Each card on the dashboard represents a specific analysis that identified potential trading opportunities. By clicking the "Show me" button you are redirected to the respective subpage in SONAR.

  • By clicking the "show me" button you are led to the section from which the alert was generated and get more details.

Portfolio

The Portfolio page shows key performance indicators on your client portfolio. Please note that this page only ever displays information on your clients, irrespective of any other settings in the application.

The top row is compromised of a set of key performance indicators, of which those with a + icon can be expanded to display more detailed information by clicking on them.

Market Data

This section contains research data retrieved from the internal UniCredit research website and it is split into two parts for Interest Rates and Exchange Rates.

  • The data is updated daily and shows the interest rates levels for the EU, US and UK.

  • The arrow under "Momentum" could be in Green, Grey or Red indicating the tendency of rising or falling exchange rates in the specified currency (UC Research source).

CLIENT ACTIVITY

This section is concerned with past client behavior and triggers that can be constructed from your clients' recent actions and activities.

Activity levels

An algorithm assignes each client an activity level from 0 (low) to 9 (high) by comparing recent and past trading activity. The algorithm takes into account all transactions within the last 3 years. A client that appears in the list here has dropped in activity level as compared to its score one week ago.

The bars are coloured in red, yellow or green depending on the activity level range the client flass in. The stripped grey segment outlines the observed drop.

Current Accounts

This page contains an overview on the netted (sum) current account balances for all clients as well as table with those clients and accounts that have recently seen above average activity (balance changes on the acccount). This page can be used to identify target clients for investment products (large positive balance), short and long term debt financing (large negative balance) and activity in currencies that might warrant hedging.

Ecommerce

This section gives an overview on the activity of your clients on any of our supported ecommerce platforms.

The charts at the top of the page show the historic development of earnings, transactions, active clients and traded volume across all platforms. Client details aggregates earnings, trdaded volmue and number of trades for each client. Trade details then shows individual trades ni the selected time frame.

Letters Of Credit

This page shows all newly issued letters of credit.

  • The specific type of the letter of credit is available by hovering over the icon in the TYPE OF LC column

Loans

This page shows all newly issued and maturing, loans as well as those with an upcoming fixing date or expiration of interest rate conditions. The latter could be a good opportunity to approach clients with unhedged rates exposure and potentially set up an interest rate swap or similar.

Pre-Settlement Risk

Clients that have almost or entirely exhausted their allocated credit lines are displayed here. Such clients could be prohibited from trading and it might be a good idea to inquire with the responsible credit officer whether their credit lines could be adjusted.

The page also contains a table with clients that haven't utilized their line (less than 10%) for an extended period of time. These clients could be approached to make use of their line by e.g. drawing loans for investement purposes.

NOTE: historic information is only available since 2019-09-09

Unusual Trades

Any unusual trades that have recently been identified are diplayed here. Unusual in the sense of the underlying algorithm here are trades that are not in line with the historic behavior of that client, e.g. a much larger notional than usual. Unsual trades could suggest a change in the client's exposure or indicate new projects or similar activity started by the client.

Hovering over the icon in the DETAILS column reveals a chart that visualizes how this trade is different from past behaviour.

  • By clicking on the icon on the top-left corner on top of the chart, the chart can be expanded for better visibilty:
  • Individual datasets can be hidden by clicking on their name in the chart's legend.

OPPORTUNITIES

This section contains various forward looking analyses that allow you to proactively interact with clients.

Client Growth

The clients shown in this section are clients who have not been trading with us for the last 12 months or that have not been trading at their full potential (compared to their peers in the same industry). It highlights opportunities to get in touch with these clients again and revive or extend the relationship.

Target Clients

All clients shown here are underperforming in some way, but concrete suggestions to remedy the situation have already been identified by SONAR. These suggestions are based on predefined conditions in the system (e.g. large amounts deposited on a current account and no recent transactions detected). You can browse thorugh the entire list of clients by using the pagination controls below the suggested three clients.

  • For each client detected there is a card summarizing the insights for growth opportunities. They include the next best product not traded with us but that would be suitable for the client, the potential of growth according to the peers activity and a specific suggested action (green area).

Clients are divided in either of "low share of wallet" or "inactive":

  • "Inactive" clients, indicated in the top-right corner, are clients who have not been trading with us for the last 12 months. Their "potential" area shows the average number of transactions their peers in the same industry have traded with us in the last year, along with the date of the last trade and the asset classes previously covered.

  • "Low share of wallet" clients are the ones that are not trading with us at their full potential.
    • The red bar shows the current share of wallet the client is trading with us;
    • the black segment shows the share the average peers are trading with us (red + black);
    • and the grey segment shows the share of the top clients that are trading the biggest share of wallet with us (red + black + grey).
  • It is possible to load more clients by clicking "load more".

Date of last trade

This table shows that last time each client in you protfolio has last traded with us. Note that clients that do not have a recorded markets transaction are not displayed here.

Foreign Exchange Monitoring

To help you stay on top of upcoming events this page features an Economic Calendar which links events with clients with an exposure in the underlying currency.

Based on the MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) analysis, the Technical analysis section shows currencies for which the market sentiment has recently changed. This analysis reveals changes in the strength, direction, and momentum of a trend.

Maturing Quotes

This page displays quotes based on requests by clients on our ecommerce platforms that ended being traded away but are now near maturity. That is the client likely traded the particular instrument with another bank, but we are in a position to follow-up on the trade close to maturity regardless.

Hedging Opportunities

Clients that are not hedging but are rather trading FX Spots in short succession are exposed to significant foreign exchange risk that they should rather hedge via Forwards, Options or Swaps. This page shows those clients with three or more FX Spot trades within a time frame of the 30 days.

Maturities

The maturites page shows maturing Markets products across asset classes. The page starts with a tree-chart that summarizes the maturities with different colours depending on the maturity date and different sizes of the boxes depending on the notional amounts. The chart helps to quickly identify large volume trades and days with a particularly large amount or volume of maturing trades. More information is available by hovering over the respective box in the chart.

The table below the chart contains the same trades but with more detailed information and in a more traditional presentation.

NOTE: The PRESENT VALUE column shows the present value of the entire strcutre, i.e. the sum of present value of all active trades in e.g. an currency management scheme. Details can be obtained by hovering over the value displayed in the column.

  • By hovering over the boxes it is possible to get more details on the maturity.
  • When clicking "toggle quick overview" the chart opens/closes.

Trading Forecast

By identifying trading patterns SONAR is able to predict future trades and for clicnt-product-currency-combinations that have exhibited some regular pattern in the past.

  • The probabilities are grouped by likelihood classes and given different colours (the greener the larger the likelihood).

EXPOSURES AND AGGREGATED DATA

This section contains various aggregated datasets on your clients' foreign exchange and commodity transactions.

Commodity Exposure

For each client that has traded a commodity product in the last 24 months this page shows the sums of the notional amounts, traded and outstanding volumes as well as the number of overall trades. Outstanding volume thereby represents the sum of notionals of all active trades. The OVERVIEW column contains several other visualizations that can be accessed by hovering over the respective info box.

  • The pop-ups available in the OVERVIEW column can be expanded by clicking the icon on the top of the top-left corner of the chart.

Foreign Exchange Exposure

For each client that has traded a foreign exchange product in the last 24 months this page shows the sums of the notional amounts, traded and outstanding volumes as well as the number of overall trades. Outstanding volume thereby represents the sum of notionals of all active trades. The OVERVIEW column contains several other visualizations that can be accessed by hovering over the respective info box.

The second table (Exposure due to liquid assets) contains a list of all current accounts denominated in non-EUR currencies with some auxiliary information.

  • It is possible to search for a specific currency followed by " bought" or "sold" in order to show all the trades with a specific direction.

Product Purchase History

This page contains highly aggregated statistics on traded products over the last 12 months by product category.

Forex Markup

The following chart gives a visual interpretation of the average markup applied for FX Spot and FX Forward in the different currency pairs, ticket sizes and maturities. On the right-hand side it is given a scale for the minimum and maximum markup applied and the respective colour grades for different levels.

  • Different currencies can be selected from the drop-down menu above the chart.
  • The more reddish chart gets the bigger is the markup applied.

ALERTS

Here users can set up custom alerts for:

  • status changes of a specific trade
  • monitoring when a client trades a specific product
  • a client's current account balance crosses a specified threshold
  • a client's earnings cross a specified threshold over a given duration
  • a client has any maturing trades in a given timespan
  • a client's activity level falls below some threshold
  • there is a trade predicted for a given client

An alert can take one of three statuses:

  1. When an alert is created the specified condition will be evaluated overnight. Until the alert has been evaluated at least once, it is in the status could not be detmined state.
  2. When an alert has been evaluated and its conditions are not met it takes the alert not triggered status. For example, an alert could be specified to trigger once the activity level of a client falls to a level of below 5. When the current activity level of that client is 5 or above, the alert would be in the alert not triggered status.
  3. Finally, when an alerts conditions are met it switches to the alert triggerd status. It will remain in this status for as long as the conditions of the alert are met.

Alerts can be modified or deleted by the user by clicking the respective buttons in the interface. When an alert is no longer valid (e.g. trade expired) it is up to the user to delete the alert.

Note: alerts are retained in the database even when deleted and can be recovered if need be. In such casese please refer to the people listed in the CONTACTS section.

DEPUTY MANAGEMENT

Deputy management is accessible via the settings button in the top navigation bar. Here you can grant another user access to your client protfolio. Users added as deputy receive full visibility on your clients, i.e. each subpage then shows information on clients by both the user himself as well as clients that are serviced by the user grating the deputy right.

  • The P-number of the colleague has to be inserted in the text box under "user id", and a validity date must be specified
  • After granting a user deputy rights they can be revoked from the panel on the right. A list of users to whom access rights have been granted is available at the top.

CLIENT VIEW

The Client View page is accessible through searching for a user in the search bar or by clicking the view button in the tooltip that appears on hovering a client's name anywhere in SONAR. The client view collects all information available on a particular client.

General Information

Some panels on this page feature a show... link that either reveals additional information or redirects to the respecitve subpage in SONAR.

  • The coloured bar shows the client's current activity level with the striped segment as the recent change (if grey it means dropped).
  • By clicking the show most recent trades link a table with the five most recent trades is revealed.

When a client is featured in any of the topic specific analyses throughout SONAR a notification regarding the particular event is also displayed in the client view

  • It is possible to expand the card by clicking on "show me" and go to the section where the alert was originated from.

The earnings panel shows the historic development of earnings with this particular client across all asset classes. The top graph gives the earnings per quatter, whereas the bottom chart gives the yearly, cummulative earnings. The blue shaded are in the background of the top graph gives the typical range of earnings with other clients in the same peer group.

  • In the upper chart, are displayed the quarterly earnings of the client, the earnings moving average and the earnings of the peer group (industry). In the lower chart are shown the quarterly cumulative earnings.

Portfolio Analysis

The portfolio analysis section contains information on active and past transactions by the client.

  • The table "active trades" shows all active instruments by the client. It mirrors the table in the Maturities page.
  • The Foreign exchange exposure panel shows the exposure of the client to specific currencies based on transactions within the last 24 months.
  • The Foreign exchange markup statistics panel details the average markup paid by the client for each currency pair and compares it with the markup paid by clients in the peer group for similar transactions. A positive value here means that the client paid more than its peers for comparable transactions.
  • The Current accounts panel shows up to date information on sight deposits held by the client.
  • The Loans panel shows the client's active loans.
  • The Commodity exposure panel mirrors the Foreign exchange exposure panel but for commodities.
  • The Foreign exchange trading channel pannel gives the fraction of the client's FX trades by platform.

Cross- and Upselling Opportunities

The final section of the Client View page summarizes various suggestions and indicators for extending the scope of services we offer to the client.

  • The Suggeted actions panel looks at all information in the Client View page and picks and presents the strongest indicators for products and services that could be offered to the client.
  • The Most popular products in peer group gives the ten most traded products in the peer group. Items in green are already covered for this client and items marked with a star are most likely to be relevant for this particular client.
  • The Potential additional foreign exchange exposure panel contains a map in which countries (representing currencies) that are already actively hedged or serviced via Markets (FX) products are displayed in blue. Colors other than grey and blue represent various degrees of certainty that the client has an exposure in a particular currency but is not yet trading that currency with CTS.
  • The Sector analysis panel gives detailed statistics on the composition of the peer group and the (FX) hedging behaviour observed therein.
  • The Estimated share of wallet panel give an approximated share of wallet per asset class for that client. This is an indication only.
